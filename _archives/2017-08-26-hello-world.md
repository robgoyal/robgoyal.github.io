### **Who am I?**

My name is Robin Goyal, and I'm currently a student at McMaster University studying Mechatronics Engineering and Management. I'm constantly asked what Mechatronics Engineering is about and the short and simple answer I give is Robotics. The formal definition from [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mechatronics) is:

<p class="message">
    Mechatronics is a multidisciplinary field of science that includes a combination of mechanical engineering, electronics, computer engineering, telecommunications engineer, systems engineering and control engineering.
</p>

In essence, Mechatronics focuses on the assimilation of mechanical, electrical, and software and the communication between these disciplines. Management provides an additional year of studies including core business concepts and its integration within engineering. 

Other than attending university, I love to play video games, hike and travel, and expand on my technical skills in my spare time. 

### **Blogging**

The purpose of creating this blog is to have a systematic procedure of documenting my programming career and some of my experiences. It also provides an avenue to improve my writing abilities, include documentation on things I've learned, and to maintain an online prescence. 

I'm going to be using it to talk about courses I'm taking, career aspects, projects, hobbies, notes and anything else that I find interesting.  

### **Career**

Although my program is highly unique and provides a high level overview on the integration of the core fields, there isn't an deep dive into any core concepts. 

For this reason, I made a goal around the beginning of this year to focus on filling in the gaps in my knowledge. This involves learning to code from scratch, understanding data structures and algorithms, solving problems and more. 

After several months of studying, I was able to build a small repertoire of skills, learn to solve problems and most importantly, I realized that learning computer science is a life-long process. Along with learning some basic skills, I also successfully landed an internship at General Dynamics Mission Systems in Ottawa as an Engineering Co-Op student. It is an incredible opportunity and I hope to talk about the process and how I received an offer at some point.
